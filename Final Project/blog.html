Blog 1: Is There Really a Greatest of All Time(GOAT) in Every Sport?

Recently I stumbled across this video about the “GOAT” debate in basketball and it got me thinking about the idea of an actual greatest of all time.

It’s crazy how good you must be to even warm the bench of the worst pro team of any sport. Lots of people remember how good the best players from their high school were, but it’s rare to see any of them go pro after college. So professional athletes have differentiated themselves from the best of the best to take up spots on a limited roster. Then there are those athletes who are even head and shoulder above other pros to the point where they stand out like Tiger Woods and Muhammad Ali. But often times, there is more than one pro better than everyone else, like Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo. So now who is the single best player? And the comparison is hard, especially when they played in different eras like LeBron James and Michael Jordan.

Soccer and basketball are the sports I’m most familiar with, so I’ll stick with them. People will argue about Messi versus Ronaldo with no clear winner. For basketball, most people will say Jordan, with LeBron a clear second. However, many will argue that LeBron is better and the debate will probably go on long after LeBron is retired. I began to wonder: is this a pointless debate? In a team sport, it’s hard to compare a player’s achievements and stats to one another. There are too many variables and factors. You might think I’m going to conclude like many others, that a GOAT debate is pointless and annoying. It kind of is, but my actual conclusion is that there is no GOAT if you have to have a debate. Team sports will always be about the team and hopefully the best team wins. Debating stats and records will always fall short compared to the outcome of an actual game.
